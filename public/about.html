About Us / Overview

The Office of the Registrar of Treaties (OROT) is established under section 13 and 14 of the Treaty Making and Ratification Act, 2012 (Rev 2018).

It is a department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs that oversees the treaty making & ratification processes of Kenya.

It is the Official custodian of records of bilateral and multilateral treaties to which Kenya is party to. It also coordinates Kenya’s responsibilities as depositary for certain treaties deposited with the Government of Kenya.

Our Mission

To ensure effective implementation of Kenya’s International Obligations in accordance with the Constitution.

Our Vision

To ensure effective implementation of Kenya’s International Obligations in accordance with the Constitution.

Functions of the Office of Registrar of Treaties

The pivotal functions of OROT are, on the one hand, to oversee the treaty making and ratification processes of Kenya, and on the other hand, to maintain an up-to-date data-base of the treaties to which Kenya is a party.

In its first role, OROT is responsible for the procedures, both main and auxiliary, relating to the making and ratification of treaties.

This includes participating in the negotiation process of treaties to ensure that the best interest of Kenya is not only protected, but the principles of international law are also adhered to. Additionally, OROT is responsible for the preparation of formal instruments relating to signature, ratification, or accession.

It also monitors the implementation of treaties ratified by Kenya.

In doing so, it provides Ministries, Departments and Agencies with legal advice on international treaty law, it also encourages them to observe and uphold their international obligations. Further, OROT ensures that the constitutional queries that may potentially arise out of the implementation of treaties are duly studied and cushioned.
